Feed on news

Welcome

Skip the hassle and make reading news fun again.

Fang's simple interface gets out of your way and lets you keep up with news at your own pace. With support for all versions of RSS and Atom, Fang works with your favorite news websites and blogs.

Tip: To return to this screen later, press the ? button at the top of the sidebar.

For the latest information head to www.GetFang.com.

Add a feed

Press the + button at the bottom of the sidebar to add a feed.

Enter the URL and press "Continue." Fang will locate and validate the feed.

Generally, you can enter website URLs such as "example.com" and Fang will be able to locate the RSS or Atom news feed, if one exists.

If Fang can't find the feed, you may have to open the site in your web browser and locate the news feed yourself. Larger news sites (BBC, NY Times, etc.) often have more than one feed.

Once Fang has found a valid feed, edit the title to your liking and press "Add Feed."

Import from other news readers

Already got your news feeds in another feed reader? No problem! Most feed readers can export your list of feeds in OPML format.

Once you have an OPML file, press the gear button at the top of the sidebar, then press the "Import" button under "OPML feed list."

Likewise, if you want to try a different feed reader, press the "Export" button to save a list of your feeds to an OPML file.

Reading a feed

Reading the news is as easy as scrolling. Select the feed in the sidebar and scroll down as you read. That's it!

As you read, Fang will place a bookmark after an item is scrolled off screen. If you change feeds or quit Fang and start it again, you'll be right where you left off.

Lost your place? Double-click the feed in the sidebar to jump back to the bookmark.

Keyboard shortcuts

Fang has keyboard shortcuts for many common operations.

↑ or ↓ Scroll up or down
→ or ← Scroll to the next or previous news item
PgUp or PgDn Scroll up or down by one screen
Home or End Scroll to the first or the bottom of the last currently loaded news item
CommandCtrlR or F5
Refresh current feed
CommandCtrlQ
Quit
